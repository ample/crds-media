Having just finished reading a pair of musical memoirs, Phil Collins' "Not Dead Yet" and Robert Hilburn's "Paul Simon: The Life", I'm full of gratitude for my musical career that never was. As a once-ambitious songwriter, my loftiest goals had me ending up somewhere between these two singer/songwriters' places in the musical firmament. But when looking at Collins' dire admissions (in the 1990s, he had #1 albums both with Genesis and as a solo artist, and created the soundtrack for Disney's massive animated hit, Tarzan. During this time he was divorced for the third time, estranged from his mistress, and a raging, daily dependent alcoholic. He says it was the worst time of his life) and Simon's troubles (his huge success in his mid-twenties made him feel alienated from his parents, his friends, his soon-to-be ex-wife, and his musical partner, Art Garfunkel. He retreated to a house in the woods and 3x/weekly therapy.) I'm reminded that God's scorecard for success is about family and faithfulness, NOT fame and fortune. So, much thanks to Phil and Paul for their warnings.

Written by Steven Manuel
